Jantar Mantar

The Jantar Mantar in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is one of the most remarkable astronomical observatories in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in 1724 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it is part of a series of five observatories he constructed across India.

Designed to observe celestial bodies and measure time with incredible precision, Jantar Mantar houses nineteen large instruments, each serving a specific astronomical function. The most famous of these is the Samrat Yantra, the world’s largest stone sundial.

Other instruments include the Jai Prakash Yantra for determining the position of stars, and the Ram Yantra for measuring altitudes. The observatory blends scientific ingenuity with architectural brilliance, as the instruments are both functional and visually striking.

The Jantar Mantar remains an educational and cultural landmark, attracting scientists, students, and tourists from around the world to explore India’s advanced astronomical knowledge of the past.
