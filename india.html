India Gate

The India Gate, located in New Delhi, is a towering war memorial built to honor the soldiers of the British Indian Army who lost their lives during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it stands at a height of 42 meters and has become one of the cityâ€™s most iconic landmarks.

Inaugurated in 1931, the monument bears the names of more than 13,000 soldiers inscribed on its surface. Its design is reminiscent of the Arc de Triomphe in Paris, yet it maintains a distinctly Indian character through its proportions and setting.

Beneath the arch lies the Amar Jawan Jyoti (Flame of the Immortal Soldier), which was added in 1971 to honor Indian soldiers who died in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971. The eternal flame is guarded by soldiers and stands as a symbol of sacrifice and national pride.

The India Gate is situated at the heart of New Delhi along the ceremonial Rajpath, surrounded by lush lawns that are popular with both locals and tourists. The area is beautifully illuminated at night, making it a stunning sight after sunset.

Today, India Gate serves not only as a memorial but also as a gathering place for celebrations, parades, and public events, continuing its role as a central point in the capital's cultural life.
