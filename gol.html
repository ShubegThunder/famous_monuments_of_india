Gol Gumbaz

The Gol Gumbaz, located in Bijapur (Vijayapura), Karnataka, is one of the largest domed structures in the world and an iconic example of Indo-Islamic architecture. Built in the 17th century, it serves as the mausoleum of Mohammed Adil Shah, the Sultan of Bijapur.

The name "Gol Gumbaz" means "Round Dome," and the monument’s massive dome, with a diameter of 44 meters, is second in size only to St. Peter’s Basilica in Rome. Remarkably, the dome stands without any supporting pillars, an extraordinary feat of engineering for its time.

Inside the mausoleum, the central chamber is famous for its unique acoustics — even the softest sound echoes multiple times, giving the structure the nickname “Whispering Gallery.”

The exterior of the monument features grand arches, massive walls, and minarets at each corner, offering panoramic views of the city from the top.

Today, Gol Gumbaz remains a celebrated architectural wonder and a must-visit site for history enthusiasts and travelers exploring Karnataka’s rich heritage.
