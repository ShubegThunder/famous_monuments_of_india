Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is one of Mumbaiâ€™s most famous landmarks and a striking example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. Located on the waterfront in the Apollo Bunder area, it was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

Standing at a height of 26 meters (85 feet), the structure faces the Arabian Sea and has become a symbolic ceremonial entrance to India. The arch is built from yellow basalt and reinforced concrete, with intricate latticework and design elements inspired by both Hindu and Muslim architectural styles.

Over the decades, the Gateway of India has witnessed many historic events, including the departure of the last British troops from India in 1948, marking the end of British rule. Today, it is a popular tourist destination, bustling with visitors, photographers, and street vendors.

In the evenings, the monument is illuminated with soft lighting, creating a stunning backdrop against the sea. From here, visitors can also take ferry rides to the Elephanta Caves and other coastal attractions.
