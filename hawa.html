Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, or "Palace of Winds," is one of Jaipur’s most enchanting landmarks and an architectural marvel of the Rajput era. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this five-story pink sandstone palace was designed by Lal Chand Ustad to resemble the crown of Lord Krishna.

Its most distinctive feature is the 953 small jharokhas (windows), intricately decorated with latticework. These windows allowed royal women to observe street festivities and daily life without being seen, in keeping with the traditions of purdah.

The design of the Hawa Mahal takes advantage of Jaipur’s breezes, with its unique honeycomb structure ensuring natural ventilation — hence its name, "Palace of Winds." The palace is constructed of red and pink sandstone, blending perfectly with Jaipur’s nickname, the Pink City.

Inside, the building has narrow corridors, small chambers, and an open courtyard. While the facade is the most photographed feature, the top floors offer breathtaking views of the bustling city and the Jantar Mantar observatory.

Today, Hawa Mahal is a must-visit destination for tourists and photographers, representing the grace, beauty, and ingenuity of Rajput architecture.
