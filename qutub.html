Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a magnificent minaret that stands at a height of 73 meters (240 feet), making it the tallest brick minaret in the world. Construction began in 1192 by Qutb ud-Din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, and was completed by his successor Iltutmish.

Built from red sandstone and marble, the Qutub Minar is adorned with intricate carvings and verses from the Quran. The structure is part of the Qutub Complex, which also houses ancient mosques, tombs, and the famous Iron Pillar of Delhi.
