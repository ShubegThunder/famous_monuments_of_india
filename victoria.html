Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial, located in Kolkata, West Bengal, is one of Indiaâ€™s most iconic landmarks and a stunning example of colonial-era architecture. Built between 1906 and 1921, it was dedicated to Queen Victoria after her death, symbolizing the height of the British Raj in India.

Designed by Sir William Emerson, the memorial combines elements of British and Mughal architecture, often referred to as Indo-Saracenic style. Made of white Makrana marble, its grand central dome, colonnades, and sculptures give it a majestic and timeless beauty.

The memorial is surrounded by lush gardens covering 64 acres, offering a peaceful escape from the bustling city. Inside, the building houses a museum with galleries showcasing historical artifacts, paintings, manuscripts, and memorabilia from the colonial period.

One of the most striking features of the memorial is the 16-foot-high bronze statue of Victory atop the central dome, which rotates with the wind.

Today, the Victoria Memorial stands as both a monument to history and a cultural hub, attracting visitors for its architectural splendor and its role in preserving India's colonial past.
