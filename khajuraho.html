Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho Temples, located in Madhya Pradesh, are one of India’s most celebrated UNESCO World Heritage Sites, famous for their intricate carvings, stunning architecture, and artistic mastery. Built between 950 and 1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty, these temples showcase a unique blend of spirituality, symbolism, and sensuality.

Out of the original 85 temples, only about 25 remain, spread over an area of 20 square kilometers. The temples are divided into three groups — the Western, Eastern, and Southern groups — with the Western Group being the most famous.

The sculptures of Khajuraho depict various aspects of life — gods and goddesses, celestial beings, warriors, musicians, dancers, and everyday scenes — as well as the renowned erotic art for which the site is often remembered.

Architecturally, the temples follow the Nagara style, with intricately carved shikharas (spires) rising high into the sky, symbolizing the connection between earth and heaven.

Today, the Khajuraho Temples are not only an important pilgrimage site but also a cultural treasure attracting tourists, historians, and art lovers from around the globe.
