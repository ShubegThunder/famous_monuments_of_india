Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the grandest royal residences in India and a prime example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. Located in the city of Mysore, Karnataka, it served as the official seat of the Wadiyar dynasty, who ruled the Kingdom of Mysore for over six centuries.

The current structure was commissioned in 1897 and completed in 1912, after a fire destroyed the old palace. Designed by British architect Henry Irwin, the palace blends Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles, creating a unique and majestic aesthetic.

The palace’s interiors are richly decorated with stained glass, intricate wood carvings, and ornate ceilings. Visitors can explore grand halls like the Durbar Hall, the Ambavilas Hall, and the Kalyana Mantapa, each adorned with fine artwork and chandeliers. The palace complex also includes beautiful gardens and temples.

Mysore Palace is particularly famous for its Dasara celebrations, when it is illuminated with nearly 100,000 bulbs, transforming it into a dazzling spectacle. This event draws thousands of visitors from across the country and the world.

Today, the palace is one of India’s most visited monuments, offering a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the Mysore royals and the rich cultural heritage of Karnataka.
