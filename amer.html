Amer Fort

The Amer Fort, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is one of the most majestic forts in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the late 16th century by Raja Man Singh I, the fort is a masterpiece of Rajput architecture, blending Hindu and Mughal styles seamlessly.

Situated on a hilltop overlooking Maota Lake, the Amer Fort is known for its large ramparts, series of gates, and cobbled paths. The fort complex is built primarily of red sandstone and marble, giving it a striking appearance against the arid landscape.

Inside, visitors can explore stunning palaces, courtyards, gardens, and halls. Highlights include the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), where the walls and ceilings are covered in intricate mirror work, and the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), where the rulers addressed the public and listened to their grievances.

The fort also offers spectacular panoramic views of Jaipur and the surrounding hills. Reaching the fort can be done on foot, by jeep, or even by elephant ride, which remains a popular tourist activity.
