Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love. Construction began in 1632 and took approximately 22 years to complete, with thousands of artisans and craftsmen contributing to its grandeur.

The Taj Mahal is an architectural masterpiece, combining elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian styles. The central dome rises to about 73 meters (240 feet) and is surrounded by four minarets. The intricate marble inlay work and symmetry are breathtaking, especially during sunrise and sunset when the monument changes hues.

Today, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors each year, making it not only a historic landmark but also a vital part of India's cultural identity.
