Sanchi Stupa

The Sanchi Stupa, located in Madhya Pradesh, is one of the oldest stone structures in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was originally commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to house relics of the Buddha.

The stupa is a large hemispherical dome that symbolizes the mound of dirt placed over the Buddhaâ€™s remains. It is crowned by a central pillar and surrounded by a stone railing with four magnificent gateways (toranas), each elaborately carved with scenes from the Buddha's life and Jataka tales.

The gateways at Sanchi are considered masterpieces of Buddhist art, depicting not only religious themes but also glimpses of everyday life in ancient India.

Over the centuries, the Sanchi complex expanded to include several smaller stupas, temples, monasteries, and pillars, making it an important center of Buddhist learning and pilgrimage.

Today, the Sanchi Stupa stands as a timeless symbol of peace, spiritual devotion, and India's rich cultural heritage, attracting scholars, pilgrims, and tourists from around the world.
